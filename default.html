Mel Spectrogram

a visual representation of audio that mimics how the human ear perceives pitch

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

Imagine you are listening to a song. A computer doesn't "hear" the song the same way you (a human) does. A mel spectrogram helps make it so a computer (including an AI) "hears" the song similar to how you (a human) hears the song.

In fact, this works for any type of audio — not just songs. A mel spectrogram can help a computer (including an AI) "hear" any type of audio the way you (a human) hears it. It could be a song. But, it could also be the sound someone talking, the sound of traffic, the sound of thunder, the sound of a dog barking, the sound of laughter, the sound of a vacuum cleaner, etc.

Visualization

Spectrograms, including mel spectrograms, are used to visualize sound — i.e., they let you see sound (rather than just hear it).

(Technically a spectrogram lets you see signals. This could include signal data from astronomy, seismology, telecommunications, etc. But, sound (i.e., audio) is a type of signal — so we will just focus on that.)

A spectrogram, including a mel spectrograms, is a way of turning sound (i.e., audio) (that you can hear) into an image (that you can see).

As it turns out, being able to see sound is not only a big deal for humans, but is also a big deal for computer (including an AI)! The reason is that this resulting image (i.e, the spectrogram, including a mel spectrograms) is a big deal is because it means you can use AI techniques designed for images on audio. For example, turning audio into a spectrogram image makes it so you can apply these AI techniques designed for images to audio: CNNs, diffusion models, vision transformers, etc.

Although, many use the log-mel spectrogram, rather than the regular mel spectrogram, when giving it to an AI.

Similar in Spirit

There are things that the mel spectrogram is similar to in spirit — meaning, they are also trying to change something to make it better reflects human perception.

A mel spectrogram transforms audio into a time–frequency representation (image) where the frequency axis is transformed (from that of a regulr spectrogram) to roughly match how humans perceive pitch differences. With a mel spectrogram, equal distances on the mel scale correspond more closely to equal perceived pitch differences than equal distances in Hz.

OKLab is a color space designed so that equal distances in the color space correspond more closely to equal perceived color differences by human vision.

From Spectrogram To Mel Spectrogram

You can create a mel spectrogram:

  1. first transform an audio signal into a time–frequency representation — this gives you a regular spectrogram
  2. and then remapping/transforming the frequency axis of the (regular) spectrogram to the mel scale — tbe mel scale tries to approximate how humans perceive pitch.
