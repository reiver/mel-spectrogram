Mel Spectrogram

a visual representation of audio that mimics how the human ear perceives pitch

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

Imagine you are listening to a song. A computer doesn't "hear" he song the same way you (a human) do. A Mel Spectrogram helps make it so a computer (including an AI) "hears" the song similar to how you (a human) hears the song.

In fact, this works for an type of audio — not just a song. A Mel Spectrogram can help a computer (including an AI) "hear" any type of audio the way you (a human) hears it. It could be a song. But, it could also be the sound someone talking, the sound of traffic, the sound of thunder, the sound of a dog barking, the sound of laughter, the sound of a vacuum cleaner, etc.
